Phage therapy — dose & outcome optimization

Predicting treatment outcomes from limited clinical data is fragile when resistant bacterial strains keep emerging mid-treatment. I estimate the full coupled system — phages, bacteria, antibodies, and multiple emergent resistant types — with calibrated uncertainty, enough to compare dosing strategies and anticipate outcomes on real data.

Measure how a complex or biological fluid resists deformation — without a conventional rheometer. I infer viscoelastic properties across a broad frequency range from optical-tweezer trajectories: in lamin networks, biological samples, and viscoelastic fluids.

Random numbers from physical noise

Physical noise can be a source of certifiable randomness. I extract random numbers from real Brownian trajectories and validate them against rigorous statistical randomness tests — bridging experimental physics and cryptographic-grade certification.

Tools developed

Software I've built and maintain — the engines behind the inference and modeling above.

B² In review · code available

A Bayesian-inference engine for biological ODE models — Hamiltonian Monte Carlo and MCMC, unified across PyMC, Turing, and JAGS so the model, not the framework, drives the work.

InvODE Live

Recovers ODE parameters from noisy data using swarm sampling and a shrink-search algorithm — reliable convergence even in high-dimensional parameter spaces where naïve fitting stalls.
